# THE PICTURE OF DORIAN GRAY — Stage 1 proposal
*The Escapable Canon — design document. No passage-writing, no UI. Awaiting approval before Stage 2.*

---

## 1. Edition identified

`novel.txt` is the **1891 book version** of *The Picture of Dorian Gray*: Wilde's preface plus twenty chapters. The identifying marks are all present: the preface itself; the chapters added in revision (III — Lord Fermor and the Agatha lunch; V — the Vane household; XV–XVIII — Lady Narborough, the opium den, Selby, James Vane's death); the toned-down Basil confession of chapter IX; the expanded chapter XI catalogues. The game is in English, in Wilde's own prose; no translator to credit. Title unit: chapters — *"a game of chapters, after Oscar Wilde."*

## 2. The object

**This volume imitates the portrait itself: a single canvas in the gilt frame Basil designed for it, hanging on the wall of the locked schoolroom at the top of the house in Grosvenor Square.** The screen is not a page. It is a dim wall — faded paper, the shadow of a purple pall gathered above the frame — and one large framed canvas on which the prose of each node appears, as if the picture had taken to keeping the record in words, which is exactly the conceit (see §3). Choices sit beneath the frame as small label-cards on the wall, in the manner of an exhibition that will never open. The novel describes the portrait but never shows it; here the inversion is exact — **the portrait shows the novel.**

**Does the game show the picture?** Only as suggestion. The canvas is rendered as a material object — ground, varnish, tone, craquelure, at the last a stain — never as a legible face. At most a darkened oval sinking into the murk behind the text. The changes are felt in the surface, not depicted, which keeps faith with Wilde's method: the horror is reported, never illustrated.

### The state-bearing element

**The condition of the canvas carries the state.** It is the one visual variable, it is diegetic (it *is* the portrait's record of the player's choices), and it carries no numbers, labels, or legend.

- **Depth on the canon path / proximity to the canon ending** drives it *darker and richer*: the ivory ground yellows toward umber, varnish thickens, a vignette of shadow creeps in from the frame edges, hairline craquelure spreads, the gilt tarnishes; in the last station a small red fleck appears low on the canvas and grows (the dew of blood on the painted hand). Immersion itself is the danger signal — as the original prose floods back, the picture ripens like a bruise.
- **Distance from the canon path** drives it the other way — *thinner*, not cleaner: paint fades toward bare ground, pencil under-drawing shows through, whole passages of the surface go unpainted. The archive exists only where the novel went; on escape corridors the picture literally runs out of paint. Documentation thinning = paint thinning; the compression dial made visible.
- On the one gated route where repentance is real (§8, ending 6), and only there, the surface does what the novel refused: it lightens — faint gold, once.

The FINIS block appears on the canvas itself; "begin again" restores the ground.

## 3. The frame-voice

**The frame-voice is the picture.** The novel supplies the license: the portrait "was conscious of the events of life as they occurred" — it received the news of Sibyl Vane's death before Dorian did. It is the one witness that sees everything and cannot lie, and it narrates in third person, in précis, in Wilde's register — with this difference from Lord Henry: **the picture pays for every epigram it makes.** That is how the voice holds Wildean wit without endorsing Wilde's most quotable corrupter: Lord Henry's paradoxes cost him nothing and are quoted at their face value; the picture's wit is a creditor's wit, always entered against an account. Epigram used to compress and to price, never to seduce. Lord Henry appears in quotation, glittering, and the frame-voice quietly appends the bill.

**Sample (three sentences):**

> The sitting lasted one afternoon; Lord Henry talked through the whole of it; the picture has been paying for the conversation ever since. It hangs where its subject put it, under a purple pall, in a room kept for broken schoolbooks and other things outgrown. What it knows of him it knows as a ledger knows a fortune — nothing of the spending, everything of the cost.

## 4. The arrow, and the formal signature

**The arrow is the paint: the portrait's accumulating record.** Dorian does not age, so the body cannot carry time; the canvas can, because paint, once laid, never lifts. Every node is anchored to a dated station of the record (a June afternoon, 18— → the Season → the theatre night → the morning after → the years, in one sweep → the ninth of November, eve of his thirty-eighth birthday → winter at Selby → the following spring), and at every node the canvas is at least as marked as it was the node before. Monotonicity is guaranteed by the fiction itself — the novel's own ending is the proof, where even an act of virtue only adds a look of cunning to the mouth. The refused escape *is* the monotone rule. Escape corridors run forward on the same calendar with the paint thinning instead of thickening; either way, no stroke is ever taken back, so the graph is acyclic by construction.

**The formal signature** that floods back as agency dies: the epigrammatic dialogue scene — talk that swallows action, Lord Henry's voice expanding to fill whole nodes — together with Wilde's aestheticized omniscient description (the studio roses, the opal dawn). In the canon endgame the choices die into single bare options — "(the nocturne continues)", "(the chapter turns)" — while chapter headings reappear and the prose runs at full length.

## 5. Node map

Spine of 36 nodes in nine stations, plus three corridors of 6 nodes each (54 total). Canon choices are always the seductive, path-of-least-resistance options. Roughly one node in four is a short node (<30 words). Notation: **[F]** flag set, **(G)** gate, **→** edge.

### Station I — a June afternoon, Basil Hallward's studio (ch. I–II)

1. **The Studio.** Basil painting, Lord Henry lounging, Dorian at the piano — all three introduced as if never met. *First choice on the first screen*: beg Lord Henry to stay (canon) / let him leave / ask Basil what he is afraid of. Converging, tints only. → 2
2. **The Garden.** Lord Henry's sermon on youth, entered late and left early (a flash, not a set piece — pacing rule). Choices: drink it in (canon) / go back inside to Basil and the easel **[BASIL]** / laugh it off. → 3
3. **The Unveiling.** The finished picture; the sense of his own beauty arriving like a revelation. Choices: three phrasings of the wish, converging — jealous, despairing, bargaining — all set **[WISH]**; **(G1)** a fourth option, *hold your tongue*, exists only if BASIL was set an hour earlier in the garden (the mechanism invisible: whom you stood beside decides whether silence is available to you). On the WISH line the scene runs on to Basil's palette-knife and Dorian saving the canvas ("it would be murder!") — with a converging alternative, *let him rip it*, which kills the engine in the studio and joins the no-WISH line (mark: slashed canvas, tints §2 element to bare ground for the rest of the run). → 4
4. **Tea.** *Short.* Choices: the theatre with Lord Henry tonight (canon) / dine with Basil **[BASIL]**. → 5

### Station II — the next day, then a month of the Season (ch. III–IV)

5. **Lunch at Aunt Agatha's.** Lady Agatha reintroduced; her Whitechapel club and the broken promise of a duet; Lord Henry's improvisation compressed to two lines. Choices: the park with Lord Henry (canon) / keep the promise — Whitechapel this afternoon → **corridor A antechamber (A1)** / call at the studio instead **[BASIL]**. → 6
6. **Curzon Street, a month later.** *Short.* Lady Henry in one sentence; "never marry, Dorian." Choices: tell Henry everything about the actress (canon) / keep her name back (mark). → 7
7. **The Finding of Sibyl Vane.** The guinea box, the Jew of the Royal Theatre compressed, Juliet aged seventeen; the kiss; the engagement. Sibyl and her world introduced fresh; her brother James, sixteen and bound for Australia, given his oath in one line of the picture's report. Choices: announce the engagement (canon) / write to Basil before anyone (tint) / press her to quit the stage at once (mark). → 8
8. **Dinner at the Bristol.** Basil's unease, Henry's theory of marriage. Choices: converging trio (boast / apologize for the suddenness / ask Basil to be glad). → 9

### Station III — the following night: the theatre (ch. VII)

9. **The Box.** *Short.* The house crowded, the gallery in shirt-sleeves. Single-choice-shaped converging pair ("the curtain rises"). → 10
10. **Three Bad Acts.** Juliet spoken like a schoolgirl's recitation; the hisses. Choices: go behind at the interval (tint) / endure to the end (canon) / leave with Henry and Basil (mark: she is alone longer). → 11
11. **The Greenroom — SET PIECE 1.** Sibyl transfigured: "I have grown sick of shadows." The fork of forks, surfaces identical in weight: **cruelty** — "you have killed my love" (canon) → 12; **mercy** — take her out of the theatre tonight, engagement standing **[MERCY]** → 14b; **mercy-marriage** — "then we marry at once" **[MERCY]**, and **(G2)** only if BASIL does the resolve survive Lord Henry's wit → **corridor B (B1)**; without BASIL it quietly decays to the 14b line.

### Station IV — that night and the days after (ch. VII–X)

12. **Covent Garden Dawn.** *Short.* The carts of lilies, the cherries cold with moonlight. Choices: look at the picture before bed / sleep first (one-node mark: when he first sees the mouth). → 13
13. **The Changed Mouth.** The touch of cruelty; the screen. Choices: write the letter of amends (canon) / go to the Euston Road at once (he arrives to drawn blinds — she died in the night; the poison was swallowed before he slept; tint of rawer grief) / dress for the club and think no more (colder tint). → 14
14. **Lord Henry at the Door.** His unread letter already on the tray, his voice on the stair — narrated in that order. Choices: let him in before reading (canon — the consolation, "a wonderful ending to a wonderful play") / read the letter alone first (grief unmediated, tint) / turn him away and go to her street (the inquest paragraph, the mother; tint). All → 15.
    - 14b. **The Morning After Mercy** (MERCY line only). Sibyl alive; guardians furious; Lord Henry's dissuasion at its most charming. **(G2)** with BASIL → **corridor B (B1)**; without, the engagement withers by consent over three sentences and the line rejoins → 15 (Sibyl lives, returns to the provinces with her mother; James sails unavenging; conditional variants downstream).
15. **Basil's Visit.** Reproach (or, on MERCY, congratulation — conditional variant), the request to exhibit, and Basil's confession of idolatry, which Dorian extracts (canon) — the love unmistakable and unnamed, in Wilde's own terms. Choices: draw out Basil's secret, then refuse the sitting (canon) / refuse everything coldly / half-move the screen and stop (one-node mark). → 16
16. **The Schoolroom.** Mrs. Leaf, the key, Hubbard's men, the purple pall. Choices: lock it upstairs (canon) / send it down to Selby (mark: the later panic of distance) / **burn it now** — a fatal fork dressed as housekeeping: the wish holds, and the household finds what the servants find in chapter XX, decades early (→ **ending 1, early variant by fire**). → 17
17. **The Yellow Book.** *Short.* Henry's parcel, paper cover slightly torn. Choices: read till the light fails **[POISONED]** (canon) / send it back unread / leave it unopened on the table (both refusals converge, POISONED unset). → 18

### Station V — the years (ch. XI, the sweep)

18. **The Sweep.** Years in one node. The chapter XI catalogues — jewels, embroideries, vestments, perfumes, instruments — dispatched in a single sentence of the picture's ledger-voice ("he collected; the picture kept the only inventory that mattered"). Choices (which hunger leads): the senses and the dens / the collections / the Roman ritual flirtation — tints only, all → 19.
19. **The Locked Room.** *Short.* The mirror held beside the canvas; the smile. Choices: the smile (canon) / stay away a whole year (tint) / try a prayer alone — nothing moves (foreshadows the gate, mechanism unseen). → 20
20. **Whispers.** The Churchill smoking-room, Berwick leaving; the ruined friends compressed to a whispered list, each a phrase. Choices: face them down (canon) / a season abroad (tint) / silence (tint). → 21

### Station VI — the ninth of November, eve of thirty-eight (ch. XII–XIV)

21. **Fog on the Pavement.** Basil, bag in hand, bound for Paris — reintroduced after long absence. Choices: pass him unseen in the fog (Basil leaves for Paris; no reveal; BLOOD stays unset; → 26 variant) / let him in (canon). → 22
22. **The Catechism — SET PIECE 2 begins.** Basil's charges, name by name. Choices: deny everything and send him to his train (→ 26 variant, as 21) / "you shall see my soul — it is your own handiwork" (canon). → 23
23. **The Schoolroom Reveal.** The candle, the grinning canvas, Basil's "pray with me — 'though your sins be as scarlet.'" Choices: **the knife [BLOOD]** (canon) → 24; **kneel and try the prayer** — **(G3)** with BASIL and not-POISONED the surface stirs for the first time and the route to **ending 6** opens (confession begun with a living witness); otherwise the words are ash, Basil leaves for Paris broken, → 26 variant; **turn him out unshown** (→ 26 variant).
24. **The Problem** (BLOOD only). The listening house; the Blue Book. Choices: Alan Campbell (canon) → 25 / the boat-train tonight → **corridor C (C1)** / walk to the station-house and give himself up (→ **ending 5, variant: the law instead of the brother**).
25. **Alan Campbell.** The refusal; the folded paper. Choices: entreat only — and when he still refuses, flee (→ **corridor C**) or write the paper after all / write it at once (canon; mark: what it costs Campbell is paid off-page in ch. XIX's one line). → 26
26. **Lady Narborough's.** *Short.* The double life at dinner; hock-and-seltzer nerves. Converging pair; conditional variants for the no-BLOOD lines (mere ennui instead of terror). → 27

### Station VII — the week after: the river (ch. XVI)

*(On MERCY-and-no-BLOOD runs this station compresses to one short node — a quiet, itching winter — and passes to Station VIII.)*

27. **The Den.** Adrian Singleton, introduced with his one-line ruin. Choices: the pipe (tint) / press money on Adrian and go (mark) / the other place on the wharf (canon). → 28
28. **The Archway** (only where Sibyl died). The revolver, the eighteen years, the lamp. Choices: the lamp-lie — "look at my face" (canon) → 29 / **"I am James Vane's man — I am he"** → **ending 5** / offer money — refused with contempt, then the lamp or the truth. 
29. **The Word at the Corner.** *Short.* The woman by the bar tells James the truth; the picture reports it as the one meeting it never saw. Choices: wire Harvey for the yacht (tint: postpones nothing) / down to Selby as planned (canon). → 30

### Station VIII — Selby Royal, winter (ch. XVII–XVIII)

30. **The Conservatory.** Gladys, Duchess of Monmouth, in two exchanges; the white face at the glass; the swoon. Converging trio (canon: the faint). → 31
31. **The Hare.** Sir Geoffrey's gun; "let it live." Choices: beg for the hare (canon) / say nothing / turn back to the house with the Duchess — the shot heard from the terrace. All converge: the beater dies; the fork's cruelty is that mercy for the hare changes nothing — Wilde's machinery, kept. → 32
32. **The Stable.** *Short.* The spotted handkerchief lifted; James Vane's face; tears of safety on the ride home (conditional variants where James never hunted: an unknown sailor, an anonymous pity). → 33

### Station IX — the following spring: the last chapters (ch. XIX–XX)

33. **The Orchard — Hetty Merton.** The village girl like Sibyl, the apple-blossoms, dawn fixed for flight. Choices: spare her (canon) / carry it through — take her away (the picture answers at once; bars ending 6) / never go back at all, unexplained (colder sparing, tint). → 34
34. **Henry's Last Dinner.** "What does it profit a man—"; the nocturne; agency dying. Single bare options begin: "(the nocturne continues)". → 35
35. **The Look — MANDATORY ENDGAME, exempt from word budgets.** The pall off; cunning in the eyes, the hypocrite's wrinkle, blood now on the painted feet. Chapter XX at full length as the choices narrow: **the knife** → **ending 1** (canon set piece: the cry, the two gentlemen in the square, the policeman, the rings); **resolve to confess** — **(G3)** with BASIL, not-POISONED and not-BLOOD → **ending 6**; on any other flags the resolve dies on the stairs and a single option remains, "(the chapter turns)" → the knife → **ending 1**.

### Corridor A — THE DUET (Whitechapel; opens at node 5)

Premise (free invention from the novel's materials — Dorian's actual competences: the piano, charm, money, and Lady Agatha's standing offer): he keeps the promise. A1 the duet before the dockers' club, and the antechamber choice — Henry's carriage waits outside with the whole glittering Season in it (return → 6), or stay for the second set (commit; corridor locks). A2 the club's winter subscription; the curate with the consumptive lung. A3 the settlement years — dated fragments, the paint thinning ("of the Whitechapel years the picture holds three entries and a subscription-list"). A4 the fever winter at the docks; what beauty is worth in a ward. A5 Lady Agatha's death; the legacy and the choice of staying poor. A6 FINIS → **ending 3**. If WISH was set, the corridor carries its quiet horror: a man of fifty at the piano, still twenty in the face, and the club's children growing old around him — conditional variants throughout.

### Corridor B — THE FOOTLIGHTS (opens at node 11 / 14b; gated by BASIL)

Premise: the plan Dorian himself announces in chapter IV, executed — buy Sibyl out of Isaacs's contract, take a West End theatre, bring her out properly; married first, at a registry office, guardians unforgiving. B1 the registry morning and Lord Radley's letter. B2 buying out Isaacs — the £50 debt, the three-year articles, comedy of commerce. B3 the venture: the novel's own cruel law holds — loved and happy, Sibyl cannot act; the house papers itself with free seats. B4 Henry's visit; the last temptation, refused or accepted (tints the finale bitter or sweet — no rejoining either way). B5 the retreat to Selby; the theatre sold at a loss nobody mentions twice. B6 FINIS → **ending 2** (the kitsch slot). Fullest text requires WISH unset (the picture never changed — it merely hung); with WISH set, a conditional variant: the picture in the attic aged honestly, and nobody looked.

### Corridor C — THE WHITE WALLS (Algiers; opens at nodes 24–25; requires BLOOD)

Premise: instead of Campbell, flight — the novel supplies the disguises in the secret press, the villa at Algiers once shared with Henry, the steamer coaling in the dark. C1 Victoria at dawn, the boat-train Basil was supposed to catch. C2 Paris under an assumed name; two vanishings in one week make the London papers (fragments begin). C3 Marseilles; the white walled-in house. C4 the years of white walls — "of the Algiers years we hold a bill of lading, a consular note, and the word of a fig-seller who never learned his name." C5 a face from the old life washed ashore — Adrian Singleton, ruined past recognizing anyone, or almost; choice: take him in / move on again. C6 FINIS → **ending 4**: the unaging man among strangers, the picture rotting alone in a locked London room, the household wages paid quarterly through a solicitor who has never seen his client.

## 6. Flags, gates, marks

**Flags (5, maximum reached):**
| Flag | Set | Selects |
|---|---|---|
| WISH | node 3, the prayer uttered | whether the canvas changes at all; its absence is a precondition of the full kitsch ending |
| BASIL | nodes 2/4/5/7, keeping faith with Basil early | gates G1, G2, G3 — silence, the marriage's survival, the prayer's sincerity |
| MERCY | node 11, Sibyl spared | Sibyl lives; no Vane hunt; large conditional-variant load at 14b/15/26/32 |
| POISONED | node 17, the yellow book read | tints the sweep; blocks G3; opens the den as habit |
| BLOOD | node 23, the knife | Campbell/flight/law stations; terror variants; blocks ending 6 at node 35 |

**Gates (3, mechanisms invisible):**
- **G1** (node 3): the option of silence before the portrait exists only if the player stood with Basil in the garden an hour before. No path shows the connection.
- **G2** (nodes 11/14b): the marriage to Sibyl survives Lord Henry's dissuasion only under BASIL; otherwise the same choice quietly decays back to the spine.
- **G3** (nodes 23/35): the prayer lands only with BASIL and not-POISONED (at 35, additionally not-BLOOD). Chosen without the flags, the same label produces the novel's own refusal — cunning in the eyes.

**Marks (one-node texture, examples):** slashed canvas (3); the withheld name (6); who is beside him at the theatre (10); when he first sees the mouth (12); the half-moved screen (15); Selby attic panic (16); Adrian's coin (27); the yacht wire (29).

## 8. Set pieces

1. **The Greenroom** (node 11, ch. VII): Sibyl's "I have grown sick of shadows" speech and the cruelty, largely in Wilde's own words. Mid-game — the player has learned what brevity feels like.
2. **Basil's Death** (nodes 22–23, ch. XII–XIII): the catechism, the reveal, the prayer, the knife.
3. **The canon endgame** (nodes 34–35 + FINIS, ch. XIX–XX): mandatory, exempt from all word budgets; the original prose floods back as the choices die.

The garden sermon (ch. II) is *not* a set piece — it falls on the second screen, so it is entered late and left early, a quoted flash inside the frame-voice.

## 9. Omissions, stated plainly

- **Chapter XI's catalogues** — jewels, embroideries, vestments, perfumes, instruments — the deepest omission in the series: one sentence.
- **Chapter III's lunch-table wit** (the Duchess of Harley, Sir Thomas, Mr. Erskine) and Lord Fermor's parentage narrative: compressed to two lines of provenance in the picture's voice.
- **Lady Henry Wotton**, Victor and Francis as characters, Mrs. Leaf's garrulity, Hubbard's comedy, the Gautier interlude, Campbell's biography: sentences or silence.
- **Mrs. Vane's theatricality and most of chapter V**: the Vane household enters as one short node's report; James's oath kept, the melodrama cut.
- **Chapter XVII's Gladys–Henry fencing** and most of chapter XIX's monologue: flashes only.
- The named ruined men (Ashton, Perth, Kent's son, Staveley's speech) fold into one whispered list; **Adrian Singleton alone is kept walking** (den, corridor C).
- Dorian's corruptions remain **unnamed**, as Wilde left them; the game gestures at ruin and invents no depravities. Basil's love stays in the register of the 1891 text — unmistakable and unnamed.

## 10. Design checks

**1. Reachability.** No orphans: every spine node lies on the canon walk or a flagged variant of it; 14b requires MERCY (reachable at 11); 24–25 require BLOOD (reachable at 23); 27–29 require the post-Sibyl or post-murder lines and are bypassed by a short bridge node on MERCY-and-no-BLOOD runs. Endings: E1 by default funnel; E2 via 2→3(silence)→11(marriage, BASIL held)→B; E3 via 5→A; E4 via 23(knife)→24(flee)→C; E5 via 28 (or 24); E6 via BASIL early + refusing the book at 17 + kneeling at 23 (or reaching 35 clean). Each verified as a concrete choice-sequence.

**2. Canon replication.** The mimicking player — keep Henry at the studio, drink the sermon, utter the wish, save the canvas, the park over Whitechapel, tell Henry everything, endure the play, cruelty in the greenroom, the letter then Henry admitted first, extract Basil's confession and refuse him, lock the picture upstairs, read the yellow book, the sweep, face down the whispers, let Basil in from the fog, show the soul, the knife, Campbell's letter, the wharf, the lamp-lie, Selby, beg for the hare, spare Hetty, the last look, the knife — reproduces the novel's trajectory and ending exactly, node for node, and every one of those options is written as the seductive default.

**3. Non-triviality.** Corridor A demands refusing Henry's open carriage twice; corridor B is flag-gated (G2) and requires a mercy chosen before the player can know its price; corridor C costs a murder first. E6 requires three separate early acts (Basil kept, the book refused, the knife refused) whose connection is never shown. The wish is nearly unrefusable without G1. The arrow has gravity: paint only accrues, dates only advance.

**4. Join consistency.** Audited merges: 14/14b→15 (Basil's visit runs reproach vs. congratulation variants on MERCY); 21/22/23-declines→26 (no-BLOOD dinner variant: ennui, not terror; Basil alive in Paris is referenced only on those lines); station VII bridge on MERCY-and-no-BLOOD (no den motive without POISONED or BLOOD — bridge node covers POISONED-only runs into 27); 32 stable scene runs the James variant only where Sibyl died — otherwise the beater is a stranger and the "safety" text is replaced (a character never met is never named there). Corridors never rejoin, so carry no join debt.

**5. Micro-chronology and causality.** Known traps already resolved in the plan: Basil's knife follows the wish-outburst inside node 3 (never precedes it); Sibyl's poison is swallowed the night of the play, so no morning fork can save her — mercy lives at the greenroom only, and node 13's "go to her now" arrives at drawn blinds; node 14 narrates the unread letter on the tray *before* the knock; node 29's meeting the picture never saw is framed as report, not witness; node 31's choices cannot avert the beater's death (the machinery is the point, and no label presupposes the outcome); no choice label anywhere names a result of the choice. The full sentence-by-sentence audit re-runs in Stage 2 on finished prose, per the prompt.
